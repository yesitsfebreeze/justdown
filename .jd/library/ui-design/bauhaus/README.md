## Origin/Era

The Bauhaus was a German art and design school founded by Walter Gropius in Weimar in 1919, later moving to Dessau and finally Berlin before its closure under political pressure in 1933. Driven by teachers like Gropius, Johannes Itten, and László Moholy-Nagy, it fused fine art with craft and industry under the credo "form follows function." Its visual language reduced design to geometric abstraction: pure shapes, primary color, and rational grids stripped of decoration.

## Defining traits

- Primary colors — red, yellow, and blue — anchored by black and white
- Pure geometric shapes: the circle, the square, and the triangle
- Asymmetric but carefully balanced grid layouts
- Geometric sans-serif typography (think Futura / Universal)
- Bold, flat blocks of solid color
- Functional and stripped down — no ornament for its own sake
- Diagonal, dynamic compositions that create movement and tension

## When to use

- Brands that want to read as modern, rational, and confident
- Editorial, posters, and covers where bold geometry carries the message
- Design systems that favor clarity and a strict, legible grid
- Cultural, architectural, or educational institutions
- Products where function and structure should be visibly celebrated
- Work that benefits from a strong, opinionated, timeless aesthetic

## Pitfalls

- Primary colors at full saturation can feel harsh or childish if unbalanced
- "No ornament" misread as "no craft" — spacing and proportion still matter
- Geometric shapes used as stickers instead of structural elements
- Over-tight tracking that hurts readability of body text
- Asymmetry without balance reads as accidental, not intentional
- Forgetting the grid: Bauhaus is disciplined, not chaotic

## Token cheat-sheet

- Palette: Bauhaus red `#E1251B`, yellow `#FFD500`, blue `#005BBB`, black `#1A1A1A`, paper `#F4F1EA`
- Border-radius: mostly `0`; circles via `border-radius: 50%`
- Shadow recipe: flat — usually none, or a hard offset like `6px 6px 0 #1A1A1A`
- Blur / elevation: flat, no blur, no soft drop shadows
- Typography: geometric sans, large weight contrast (e.g. 400 vs 800), tight tracking on display headings
- Spacing: 8px base grid, generous gutters between blocks
